## Recommended Next Execution Step

Run one bounded **Discovery-to-Decision cycle**：

1. Define candidate wedges and first-value moments。
2. Interview / concept-test 5-10 relevant users as a directional discovery sample。
3. Record current alternatives、trigger moments、trust issues and return intent。
4. Ingest source-linked evidence into the PM Workflow。
5. Generate an evidence matrix and decision memo without labeling the sample as market validation。
6. Founder approves、rejects or defers the primary wedge and first-value moment。
7. Update v1 scope and proceed to UX / technical Build Readiness only after this decision。

## Implications for the AI-Native PM Workflow Dogfooding Scope

The PM Workflow should initially prove one narrow capability：

> Convert fragmented discovery inputs into a traceable founder decision and synchronized product artifacts。

Initial outputs：

- Research evidence repository。
- Segment / JTBD evidence matrix。
- First-value option comparison。
- Decision memo with confidence and counter-evidence。
- Updated decision log、risk register、scope recommendation and backlog。
- Weekly founder update showing what changed and why。

Explicitly out of scope for the first slice：

- Full GitHub / engineering automation。
- Automated launch-readiness scoring。
- Automatic market-validation claims。
- Automated pricing or monetization decisions。
- Ingestion of sensitive user content without an approved data policy。

Evidence of workflow usefulness：

- Reduced synthesis and document-update time。
- Clear traceability from evidence to decision。
- Conflicts and gaps remain visible。
- No silent upgrade of hypotheses into facts。
- Founder can make and communicate a decision faster while retaining accountability。
