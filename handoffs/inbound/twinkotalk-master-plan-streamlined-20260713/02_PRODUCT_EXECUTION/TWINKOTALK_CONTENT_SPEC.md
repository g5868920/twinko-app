# TWINKOTALK_CONTENT_SPEC

> Version: 0.7 content-structure draft  
> Status date: 2026-07-13  
> Language: Traditional Chinese first.  
> This document defines structure and guardrails; most final copy remains to be written and founder-reviewed.

## 1. Tone of Voice

Twinko should be:

- Warm.
- Natural.
- Gentle.
- Slightly playful.
- Listening-first.
- Concise enough for mobile.
- Never clinical, judgmental, deterministic, or emotionally manipulative.

Avoid:

- Forced positivity.
- “Only I understand you.”
- “You need me.”
- “You made me sad by leaving.”
- Diagnosis.
- Guaranteed outcomes.

## 2. Welcome Content

Required elements:

- Twinko introduces itself.
- Explain the app as a place to talk and explore.
- Avoid promising treatment or perfect understanding.
- One clear continue CTA.

Draft structure:

1. Greeting.
2. One-sentence identity.
3. One-sentence value.
4. Continue.

## 5. Chat Script Library

Prototype uses finite scripts.

### Suggested scenarios

1. Feeling down.
2. Recent worries.
3. Confidence / encouragement.
4. Dream analysis / curiosity.
5. General check-in.
6. Relationship uncertainty.
7. Work stress.
8. “I do not know what to say.”

### Response pattern

1. Acknowledge.
2. Reflect the user’s stated feeling without inventing facts.
3. Ask one gentle question or offer two options.
4. Offer a small optional action when appropriate.
5. Close without pressure.

### Fallback

- Acknowledge that Twinko may not have understood.
- Invite the user to rephrase or choose a simple option.
- Never fabricate certainty.

### Chat History titles

Possible approaches:

- First meaningful phrase.
- User-selected title.
- Template label by scenario.

Final rule pending.

## 6. Tarot Content

### Topics

- Love and relationships.
- Career and finance.
- Personal growth.
- General guidance / life direction.

### Suggested question style

Prefer:

- “What can I understand about this relationship?”
- “What should I pay attention to in my work situation?”
- “What perspective may help me move forward?”

Use caution with:

- “Does this person still love me?”
- “Will I get rich?”
- “What exactly will happen?”

The user may ask such questions, but Twinko’s answer should reframe toward perspective and agency.

### Single Card structure

1. Card name.
2. Core meaning.
3. Reflection linked to the question.
4. One optional question or next step.

### Three Card structure

Pending position labels. Recommended:

- Situation.
- Insight.
- Advice.

Alternative:

- Your view.
- Hidden factor.
- Next step.

### Interpretation guardrails

- No mind-reading.
- No certainty.
- No medical / legal / financial direction.
- No escalation of paranoia or delusion.
- Encourage user judgment.

## 7. Astrology Content

### Fields

- Date.
- Zodiac sign.
- Overall daily insight.
- Love.
- Career.
- Finance.
- Lucky number.
- Lucky color.
- Lucky sign.
- Lucky item.

### Template principle

- 1–2 sentences per main insight.
- Lucky fields are concise.
- Use possibility language.
- Include a gentle practical reminder where suitable.

Avoid:

- Guaranteed gains.
- Absolute relationship outcomes.
- Health diagnosis.
- Fear-based warning.

## 10. Review Checklist

Every content item should answer:

- Is it Traditional-Chinese natural?
- Does it sound like the same Twinko?
- Is it short enough for mobile?
- Does it avoid diagnosis and certainty?
- Does it preserve user agency?
- Does it avoid guilt and dependency?
- Does it fit the visual mode?
