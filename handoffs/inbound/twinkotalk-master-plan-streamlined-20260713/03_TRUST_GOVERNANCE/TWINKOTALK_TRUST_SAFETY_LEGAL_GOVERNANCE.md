# TWINKOTALK_TRUST_SAFETY_LEGAL_GOVERNANCE

> Status date: 2026-07-13  
> This is a product-governance baseline, not legal advice. Professional review is required before public beta / launch.

## 1. Product Classification and Claims

TwinkoTalk is currently a consumer companion and reflection product.

Do not claim:

- Diagnosis.
- Treatment.
- Therapy.
- Prevention or cure.
- Clinical improvement.
- Replacement for a professional.
- Accurate prediction of the future.
- Knowledge of another person’s thoughts.

## 2. Emotional-Support Boundary

Twinko may:

- Listen.
- Reflect the user’s stated feeling.
- Ask gentle questions.
- Offer optional grounding, breathing, rest, or small actions.
- Encourage contact with a trusted person or professional.

Twinko must not:

- Diagnose.
- Pressure the user to remain in the app.
- Imply exclusive understanding.
- Say the user is responsible for Twinko’s emotions.
- Hide high-risk situations behind entertainment.

## 3. CBT and Mindfulness

General reflective, grounding, breathing, and perspective-taking patterns may be used as wellness design inspiration.

The correct governance is not “use CBT but do not say so.” Instead:

- Document internal methods.
- Separate general wellness from structured clinical intervention.
- Avoid clinical claims.
- Obtain professional review if using recognizable protocols.
- Define contexts where the product must stop or redirect.

## 4. Low Mood and High-Risk Situations

### Low mood

- Listen first.
- Ask what the user wants: listening, comfort, perspective, or a small action.
- Avoid forced optimism.
- Do not infer a diagnosis.

### High risk before public beta

A policy is required for:

- Self-harm / suicide.
- Violence.
- Abuse or domestic violence.
- Severe mental-health crisis.
- Delusion or paranoia.
- Minor safety.
- Medical emergencies.

The prototype does not use an LLM, but public beta cannot rely on a simple keyword-only response.

## 5. Tarot and Astrology

### Approved framing

- Reflection.
- Entertainment.
- Perspective.
- Symbolic prompts.

### Prohibited framing

- Guaranteed outcome.
- Mind-reading.
- Medical advice.
- Legal advice.
- Financial instruction.
- Crisis decision-making.
- Reinforcing delusion.

## 6. Dependency and Engagement

- No guilt streaks.
- No punishment for absence.
- No “Twinko is sick / sad because you left.”
- No relationship loss for taking a break.
- No distress-triggered paywall.
- No claim that more app time means better wellbeing.
- User can leave, pause, delete, and control contact.

## 7. Data and Privacy

Prototype fields:

- Name.
- Birthday.
- Gender.
- Chat History.

Production questions:

- Is each field necessary?
- Is gender required?
- Can the user choose “prefer not to say”?
- Can only zodiac sign be stored instead of full birthday?
- How long are chats stored?
- Can users delete individual chats?
- Is data sent to AI providers?
- How is consent presented?
- How are export and deletion handled?

## 8. Age Policy

Recommended working direction: adults first.

Before serving minors, the product would need:

- Age-appropriate content.
- Parental / guardian considerations.
- Stronger privacy controls.
- Crisis and exploitation protections.
- Region-specific legal review.

## 9. Notifications and Proactive Contact

Future notifications require:

- Explicit opt-in.
- Frequency control.
- Quiet hours.
- Topic control.
- Pause / disable.
- No guilt language.
- No sensitive-emotion targeting without clear consent.

## 10. Content and IP

- Twinko art must be original.
- External visual references are mood references only.
- Music requires rights.
- Tarot card art should be original or properly licensed.
- Astrology and spiritual content should not copy proprietary content.
- Creator and professional contributions require clear rights and attribution.

## 11. Marketing Claims

Avoid:

- “Treats depression.”
- “Better than therapy.”
- “Knows exactly how you feel.”
- “Predicts your future.”
- “Never leaves you.”

Prefer:

- “A warm place to talk and reflect.”
- “Daily insights for reflection and entertainment.”
- “A character-led companion experience.”

## 12. Monetization Ethics

- Do not monetize crisis access.
- Do not use emotional distress to force purchase.
- Clearly state what is free and paid.
- Avoid deceptive scarcity.
- Cosmetics and entertainment are safer scarcity targets than emotional support.

## 13. Governance Gates Before Public Beta

- Privacy policy.
- Terms.
- AI disclosure.
- Safety behavior policy.
- Crisis-resource design.
- Tarot / astrology disclaimer and content review.
- Age policy.
- Data deletion.
- Vendor and content-rights review.
- Marketing-claims review.
