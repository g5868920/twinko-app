## 3. Execution Sequence

1. Canonical review.
2. Resolve open micro-decisions.
3. Lock PRD.
4. Lock screen inventory.
5. Lock character and visual system.
6. Complete content minimum set.
7. Produce prototype.
8. Run QA.
9. Conduct small review sessions.
10. Decide the true MVP scope.

## 4. Change Control

A prototype-scope change requires:

- Decision Log entry.
- PRD change.
- Screen Inventory change.
- QA update.
- Backlog status update.

Ideas do not become requirements merely because they are attractive.

## 5. Weekly Review Template

- What changed.
- What completed.
- What is blocked.
- Decisions needed.
- Scope changes.
- Risks.
- Next priority.

## 6. Definition Hierarchy

- **Prototype** — validates flow and experience.
- **MVP** — minimum product suitable for beta learning with safety, analytics, reliability, and policy requirements.
- **Public launch** — store-ready, legally reviewed, supported, and operational.

## 7. Production Readiness Gate

- PRD approved.
- Screen Inventory approved.
- Visual system approved.
- Content ready.
- Open decisions resolved.
- QA ready.

## 8. Post-Prototype Review Questions

- Is Twinko memorable?
- Do modes feel unified?
- Does Chat feel warm even without LLM?
- Does Tarot feel ritual-based rather than mechanical?
- Is Astrology clear and worth revisiting?
- Which mode deserves MVP investment?
- Which screens create confusion?
- Which deferred features should remain deferred?
